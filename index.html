Malaysia Tourism Recovery and Travel Patterns

Malaysia’s tourism sector is recovering after recent disruptions. This dashboard explores how recovery differs across time, visitor markets, expenditure patterns, and Malaysian states.

Story Overview

The visualisation follows three questions: when did tourism activity strengthen, where did visitors come from, and which states benefited most from the recovery?

Part 1: National Recovery Trend

Tourism recovery begins with the timing of visitor arrivals across 2024.

1. Monthly Visitor Arrivals

Visitor arrivals generally increased across 2024, with stronger travel activity toward the end of the year.

Key insight: December recorded one of the strongest visitor arrival peaks.

Part 2: Visitor Markets and Spending

The next section explains who visits Malaysia and how tourism money is spent.

2. Top Source Countries

Visitor arrivals are heavily concentrated among neighbouring Asian countries, highlighting Malaysia's dependence on regional tourism demand.

Key insight: Singapore contributed nearly 20 million visitor arrivals, making it Malaysia's largest source market in 2024.

3. Tourism Expenditure Breakdown

Key insight: Shopping represented the largest tourism expenditure category, accounting for almost half of total visitor spending in 2024.

Part 3: State-Level Hotel Recovery

Hotel occupancy and guest numbers reveal that recovery is uneven across Malaysian states.

4. Occupancy Comparison

Hotel occupancy improved in most selected states between 2024 and 2025, suggesting continued recovery in accommodation demand.

Key insight: Pahang and Kuala Lumpur recorded some of the strongest hotel occupancy performance.

5. Recovery Intensity Heatmap

The heatmap gives a compact view of how occupancy differs by state and year, making it easier to compare recovery intensity.

Key insight: Pahang recorded the strongest hotel occupancy across both years.

6. Hotel Guest Growth

The slope chart compares hotel guest numbers between 2024 and 2025, showing whether selected states continued to grow during the recovery period.

Key insight: All selected states recorded higher hotel guest numbers in 2025, with Kuala Lumpur remaining the largest hotel guest centre.

Part 4: Spatial Pattern of Recovery

Maps show both where hotel occupancy is strongest within Malaysia and where high-value visitor markets are located globally.

7. Malaysia Choropleth Map

The choropleth map highlights where hotel occupancy is strongest geographically across Malaysian states.

Key insight: Darker states indicate stronger hotel occupancy performance in 2025.

9. Visitor Receipts Symbol Map

The proportional symbol map shows where Malaysia’s highest-value visitor markets are located globally.

Key insight: Singapore, China and Indonesia generated the largest tourism receipts in 2024.

Part 5: Transport and Connectivity

Transport and air connectivity explain how visitor movement supports Malaysia’s tourism recovery.

8. Transport Mode Share

Transport mode data shows how visitors physically entered Malaysia, revealing the importance of land-border travel.

Key insight: Land transport accounted for the majority of visitor arrivals, highlighting the importance of cross-border travel from neighbouring countries.

10. Air Connectivity Recovery

Air connectivity recovery remains strongest in nearby Asian markets, though emerging regions such as Central Asia experienced the highest growth rates from a smaller base.

Key insight: Southeast Asia provided the largest seat capacity, while Central Asia recorded the fastest growth in available seats.

Conclusion

Malaysia's tourism recovery remained strong throughout 2024 and into 2025. Visitor arrivals increased over the year, Singapore continued to dominate as the largest source market, shopping represented the largest expenditure category, and hotel occupancy improved across most states. Spatial and transport analyses further show that recovery was supported by strong regional demand and improving connectivity.es.
